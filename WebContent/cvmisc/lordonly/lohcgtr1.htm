Hal Chiasson on Lead Guitar
and Vocals
Electric and Acoustic Guitars

 

What guitars do you use mainly?

I really like Fender and Ibanez instruments, they are well made and they sound good, a real value. For 6-strings, I use 2 American Standard Fenders, the blond one I put The Point System on and a green also has future upgrades and augmentations in mind. A blue Ibanez Talman that I put a Floyd Rose on, a blue Ibanez Custom - the Satriani model and a red Ibanez Reverse Head Stock Custom that I have further modified. My 12-string electric is a blue Yamaha Pacifica.

How does playing an electric 12-string compare to an electric 6-string?

You know, I don't really notice too much of a difference, but then again I don't play the 12-string very much live. I have found that soloing is different on a 12-string, but the sound is unique and worth the effort. I use my Roland VG-8 for a lot of the 12 string stuff live, it really gets the job done.

What guitars are you considering adding to your setup?

I have been looking for a suitable doubleneck guitar that would fill the roles I would need it to, but then again, I just recently got a Roland's VG-8 guitar system and the Roland GR-1 Guitar synth and I believe that they will probably cover everything I may need and then some. Then again, you can't beat the visuals of a doubleneck during a performance, especially with John's bass doubleneck and since Brian has one too, that would be six necks at once!

I am also looking seriously at Brian Moore guitars - those instruments are really well made.

What kind of acoustic guitars do you play?

I have an Ovation Piezo Pickup Acoustic 6-string that I use on our recordings as well as live. The piezo has an excellent tone, and mixed with the mikes that John used for recording it, I felt we got an excellent acoustic tone on the recording, especially for some of the rhythm parts of Time Out and Images.

How do you play acoustic live during an electric set?

It is a pain to lug around so many instruments, so I used to sometimes cheat by using a Rockman Electric to Acoustic floor stomp box, but with the purchase of the VG-8, I won't really have to worry about it anymore - the acoustic guitar modeling is excellent in that unit.

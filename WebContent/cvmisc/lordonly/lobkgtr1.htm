What guitars do you use mainly?

My primary instrument is a black & gold 1989 Gibson Les Paul Custom, which I sometimes call "Lester Polfus." Most people don't know it, but that's Les Paul's real name. I got it barely used from a guy in NYC in '92-- those Internet newsgroups come in handy sometimes. Generally, I'm not afraid to make modifications to instruments; anything that improves the sound or playability is fair game. This one has had several mods, including a Roland GK-2 hex pickup drilled into the body, which I'm now replacing with the RMC piezo system. It contains the PolyDrive I and Pow'rBridge 'G' electronics, which work a lot better than the Roland-style hex pickups.

The secondary guitar is a metallic green Hamer Eclipse 12-string, with Seymour Duncan mini- humbuckers. I was looking for a good 12 for a while, and fell in love with it right away. It has a set neck and fixed bridge like the LP, and because of the humbuckers, it doesn't mow you down with the typical 12-string treble. In fact, you can get a nice mandolin-ish sound from it if you want. Right now I play it on Spiritual Animal and Sink Or Swim, plus other bits here and there on the recordings (Fear, Predaotr of Dreams, and Nosferatu).

Of course I have to have a Strat; mine is a Squier Pro-Tone, with stock single-coils, a Seymour Duncan humbucker and Floyd Rose tremelo.

The latest (final?) one is a Carvin 6/12 doubleneck that I got from an Internet dealer in Dallas. It's very versatile because each neck has a phase switch and coil taps on both pickups. With those, tone knobs and pickup selectors, I can dial in a pretty good imitation of a Les Paul, Tele, or the Hamer 12. That makes this instrument great for playing live; it's more awkward to play than the others but it's necessary to be able to duplicate what's on the records. To make it complete, I'll be adding a GK-2 synth pickup to the 6 neck pretty soon.

How does playing an electric 12-string compare to a 6-string?

The 12 is more physically demanding, since you're pressing down, sliding, & bending twice as many strings. You sacrifice some subtlety in dynamics & vibrato, in exchange for that wonderful tonal richness you gain. Also, when you bend notes on a 12, the two strings don't rise in pitch the same amount-- because of differences in displacement & tension-- so you get a strange "warbling" sound. No 12-string simulator can do that. The main drawback to 12's is the inevitable tuning problems... [laughs]

What guitars are you considering adding to your setup?

I still have my first guitar, an Epiphone Strat clone, which I might do something with. And I would love to have a custom Klein electric sometime in the future. Ergonomically, they are among the best I've played, and I'd like to have something really different: single-coil, chambered body, Steinberger tremelo, headless... I recently played this Conklin Arch-top Guitar at the NAMM show in Nashville. That guitar was one of the best guitars I ever played. Who knows, John may not be the only Conklin player in this band for long.

That slide guitar is pretty cool-- tell me about it.

It's called a Smith Melobar, with 8 strings and a Bill Lawrence humbucker. I discovered this at the '97 winter NAMM expo and was amazed at how great they sound, especially considering the very reasonable price. I highly recommend this for anyone thinking about dabbling in some form of slide; call 1-800-MELOBAR for info. I'm still pretty much a novice on it, but it's easy to get some cool sounds from it right away.

How do you tune something like that?

It came with "Hawaiian tuning" but I've changed it to a straight A minor (E-A-C-E-A-C-E-A, low to high). There are probably dozens of different ways to do it. I fooled around with a number of tunings but this one was simple and gave me an open position for Am, and easy Dm (5th fret) and Em (7th) chords. Plus you have two octaves of each note in the triad and one lower and one higher.

How does playing slide differ from normal guitar, for you?
     

Obviously there's the different tuning, so it's difficult to improvise anything (with my normal patterns, that is). On the other hand, if you miss a note it's pretty easy to quickly slide up or down a bit till you land on a good one! I've done some standard-tuning glass slide on the LP, but this is completely different: holding a steel bar with an over-hand grip, fretless, about 2 cm. above the board. I like this better than regular slide. You can hear it in Daedalus and Images, and a bit near the beginning of Versatile Residue.

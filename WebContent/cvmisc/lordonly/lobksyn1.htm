How do you get those weird sounds out of your electric guitars?

With the Roland GR-1 guitar synthesizer, which I've been using since 1993. What a great invention! I used to wonder how we'd do without a full-time keyboardist, but with this synth plus Hal's keyboards & John's pedals, we have it covered. The GR-1 has pretty good sounds (based on the Sound Canvas) & extensive tone-shaping controls, although the new GR-33 uses the JV-1010 engine so it's probably better. I usually keep the synth on even when it's not featured, to add a background texture to my guitar tone. The fluegel horn in the Devil's Advocate verses is an example of this. I haven't been completely satisfied with the Roland pickup's tracking, though, so I'm getting a custom piezo/synth system (RMC Pow'r Bridge & Poly-Drive I).

 

How does the guitar synth affect how you come up with your parts?

It doesn't really affect composition, but it sometimes helps determine whether Hal or I will take a certain solo. Hal does most solos in Devil's Advocate & Predator Of Dreams, for instance, since I need to be playing the guitar synth in those parts. It's also handy for recording MIDI into Cakewalk for new song ideas.

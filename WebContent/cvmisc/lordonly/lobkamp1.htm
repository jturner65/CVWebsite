Ok, so what is your stage amp rig?

Marshall JMP-1 preamp, Marshall 9200 power amp, Sonic 2-x-12 cabinets, T.C. Electronics G-Force and Digitech TSR-24s effects units, Roland GR-1 guitar synth and expression pedal, Vox wah pedal, Korg DTR-1 strobe tuner (very important), and hopefully a pint of Guinness.

 

How much of the vocals do you perform?

I'm the secondary singer: when there are two voices it's usually Hal & me; John often joins in for a third. Hal & I trade off lead phrases sometimes, and I sing lead on two songs so far: Wounded & Sherlock. We all work on the vocal arrangements. Since I sound kind of mellow I will often record all three vocal parts, and then Hal doubles the high and middle ones, and John doubles the low one.

How do you prepare your voice for a live show?

By not screaming at my cats much that day. And I try to sing some every day, mostly in the car by myself, singing with the radio or a CD... [laughs] I make up harmonies on the fly, which helps me lock onto pitches more easily when we're playing. Seriously, singing harmonies to the radio is a good way to train your ear, if you do it enough. After a while a song can even sound strange if you're not adding your harmony...

 

Oh by the way, what kind of dying animal do I hear moaning in the backgrounds of Wounded and Spiritual Animal?

Mmm... that's my clarinet playing. Glad you liked it!

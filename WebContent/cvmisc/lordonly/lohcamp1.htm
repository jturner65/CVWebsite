Hal Chiasson on Lead Guitar

Lead Vocals and Amplification Rig

 

You do most of the lead vocals. Do you ever find playing and singing difficult?

Initially, when we begin working on a new song, I usually focus more on the vocal, since it will be what the guitar, as well as everything else, will be serving. Besides, I am going to have to end up doing both things, and the harder guitar parts I can always let Brian do (laughs). Once I have decided on an idea, it's practice, practice, practice until I end up getting it tight.

What are the most difficult parts or songs you do?

From the first note to the last. (laughs) Actually, there are no really standouts for me, except maybe Devil's Advocate and the rest of the songs in that series due to their complexity. Usually by the time we play out, I feel comfortable enough with the material that I am not dreading any of it, at least not too much.

 

How do you prepare your voice for a live show?

I build up my voice by practicing along with tapes and CD's of us the weeks before the show, but right before a show I usually just hum a few of the notes that I will later be singing - I try to warm up my ear without wearing out my voice.

What is your stage amp rig?

I use a Line 6 AX2 212 combo amp; it has various distortions and effects built in, I sometimes back that up with my Ampeg 4x12 and ADA 2x12 cabinets. I run the outputs from my Roland VG-8 and GR-1 synths into the aux inputs on the AX2, and get a world of sound. And don't forget the good old SM-58. And don't forget the good old SM-58.
